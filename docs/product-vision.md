# Product Vision

## Purpose

Define the product problem, intended user value, decision workflow, and product boundaries of GameMarketIntel.

## Vision

GameMarketIntel is a decision-support platform for Game Producers and small studios.

It transforms fragmented public game and market information into an organized research starting point that helps producers evaluate the commercial viability of game ideas.

The platform should reduce the time required to move from an initial concept to a grounded discussion with teams, publishers, and stakeholders.

## Central Product Question

Each product increment should contribute to answering:

> Does this game idea deserve to advance to the next stage of evaluation or production?

GameMarketIntel does not make that decision automatically.

It provides transparent, contextualized, source-aware evidence so that the producer can make a more informed decision using the realities of the studio, team, budget, schedule, audience, and strategy.

## Problem Statement

Early-stage game market research is fragmented.

A producer may need to move between:

- search engines;
- official game websites;
- digital storefronts;
- trailers and gameplay videos;
- positive and negative player reviews;
- publisher and developer pages;
- sales announcements;
- regional news;
- analyst estimates;
- player and engagement trackers.

Generic search results often prioritize famous games and editorial rankings.

They may help discover popular titles, but they rarely provide enough context to evaluate a market niche, identify less-visible comparable products, understand typical commercial performance, or locate the most relevant original sources efficiently.

The manual process remains possible, but it is slow, repetitive, and vulnerable to survivorship bias.

## Target User

The primary user is a Game Producer or small-studio decision-maker performing early product and market research.

Secondary users may include:

- studio founders;
- product managers;
- market analysts;
- consultants;
- developers participating in concept validation.

## Product Value

GameMarketIntel should help the user:

- discover comparable games beyond only the best-known successes;
- understand the basic shape of similar products;
- locate official and relevant external research sources;
- evaluate commercial performance across multiple comparable games;
- distinguish official values from estimates;
- understand source reliability and data limitations;
- reduce uncertainty before committing significant production resources;
- prepare stronger evidence for stakeholder discussions.

## Decision Workflow

```text
Game idea
    ↓
Find comparable games
    ↓
Understand similar products
    ↓
Open organized research references
    ↓
Evaluate commercial evidence
    ↓
Assess market context
    ↓
Support a stakeholder decision
```

### Find Comparable Games

The producer identifies games related by name, genre, platform, release context, and future similarity criteria.

### Understand Similar Products

The producer reviews characteristics such as:

- genres;
- platforms;
- release dates;
- publisher and developer, when available;
- descriptions;
- official media and gameplay references.

### Open Organized Research References

GameMarketIntel should help the producer reach relevant original or specialized sources more quickly.

Possible references include:

- official game website;
- official publisher or developer page;
- Steam or other digital storefronts;
- official trailers;
- gameplay videos;
- review aggregators;
- source articles and reports.

The platform organizes access to these sources. It does not replace them.

### Evaluate Commercial Evidence

Sales are the highest-priority indicator because they provide direct evidence of commercial market response.

Priority order:

1. units sold;
2. revenue;
3. estimated owners;
4. downloads;
5. active players;
6. peak concurrent players;
7. reviews, wishlists, followers, and other complementary indicators.

The availability and usefulness of each metric depend on game type and business model.

For online games, active-player and concurrent-player observations may provide important retention or engagement context.

For free-to-play games, downloads alone must not be treated as equivalent to commercial success.

### Assess Market Context

The producer should eventually be able to compare:

- successful and less-successful comparable games;
- famous titles and less-visible indie products;
- performance distributions rather than only top sellers;
- platform and regional differences;
- release timing;
- genre saturation;
- data freshness;
- source confidence.

This reduces survivorship bias and helps distinguish exceptional outliers from more typical market outcomes.

## Product Boundaries

GameMarketIntel is not:

- a replacement for official game websites;
- a replacement for Steam or other stores;
- a review platform;
- a gameplay-analysis engine;
- a guarantee of commercial success;
- an automated production decision-maker;
- an oracle that labels ideas as universally good or bad.

GameMarketIntel is:

- a research starting point;
- a comparable-games discovery experience;
- a source-aware evidence organizer;
- a commercial research aid;
- a decision-support tool.

## Evidence Principles

Every future market metric should preserve, when available:

- source;
- reliability;
- official or estimated nature;
- measurement meaning;
- period;
- region;
- platform;
- collection date;
- known limitations.

Estimated values must never be presented as official facts.

A non-official source may still be useful when it is recognized by industry professionals and its uncertainty is communicated clearly.

## Success Direction

The long-term product should be judged not only by how much data it stores, but by whether it reduces the time and effort required for a producer to complete a useful first market-research pass.

A meaningful product outcome is:

> The producer reaches a better-supported next-step decision with fewer fragmented searches and less manual organization.
